## **Overview**
Once a peaceful farming community known for golden wheat fields that stretched to the horizon, Goldmeadow now lives under the watchful eye of the Vigilant Order. When the Maelstrom erupted, the Order moved swiftly to "secure vital food supplies for the faithful"‚Äîwhich meant occupying the village with armed templars and seizing control of harvests. The villagers are divided: some grateful for protection from chaos, others bitter about losing autonomy and seeing their food requisitioned while they go hungry.

The village name comes from its fields‚Äîacres of wheat that glow like spun gold in late summer. Now those fields feed Goldspire's garrison and Order outposts across the region, with locals receiving barely enough to survive.

## **Geography**

### **Location & Layout:**
- **8 miles from Goldspire:** Half-day walk, 2-hour ride
- **River Valley Setting:** Fertile soil, reliable water from small tributary of [[üåä Silverflow River]]
- **Flat Terrain:** Easy farming, easy to patrol, no hiding places
- **Single Road:** Main path to Goldspire, heavily traveled and watched
- **Village Center:** Clustered houses around central square
- **Farmsteads:** Scattered homesteads in surrounding fields

### **Key Areas:**

**Village Square:**
- Small market (heavily taxed by Order)
- Village well (guarded, water rationed)
- Temple/Church (Order-controlled, mandatory services)
- Order Garrison Building (former village hall)
- Public Notice Board (Order proclamations, curfews, quotas)

**Farmlands:**
- **West Fields:** 400 acres of grain (wheat and barley)
- **East Pastures:** Grazing land for 200+ cattle, 500+ sheep
- **North Orchards:** Apple and pear trees (small)
- **South Gardens:** Vegetable plots for each household

**Order Installations:**
- **The Garrison:** 40 templars stationed permanently
- **Watchtowers:** Four wooden towers overlooking approaches
- **Checkpoint:** Inspects everyone entering/leaving village
- **Grain Storehouse:** Heavily guarded, holds requisitioned harvests
- **Stables:** Horses for patrols and messengers

## **Life Under Occupation**

### **Order Control Measures:**

**Food Requisition:**
- 70% of harvest goes to Order
- Livestock taken "for the faithful"
- Villagers receive bare minimum rations
- Refugees get even less (creates tension)

**Security Protocols:**
- Dawn-to-dusk curfew
- Checkpoint inspections on all roads
- Random house searches for contraband
- Weapons confiscated from civilians
- Travel permits required to leave village

**Religious Requirements:**
- Mandatory temple attendance twice weekly
- Order doctrine lessons for children
- Public prayers before harvest and work
- "Cleansing" rituals to prevent corruption
- Confession to Order priests (used for intelligence)

**Labor Demands:**
- Villagers must work Order-directed quotas
- Extra labor "volunteered" for Goldspire projects
- Men conscripted for defense work
- No wages‚Äîpayment is "protection"

### **Daily Village Life:**

**Dawn (5 AM):** 
- Curfew lifts, templars patrol
- Farmers head to fields under watch
- Children to mandatory Order lessons
- Ration distribution at garrison

**Day (6 AM - 6 PM):**
- Farming work under templar supervision
- Market operates (limited, taxed heavily)
- Order patrols constantly visible
- Villagers whisper complaints when safe

**Evening (6 PM - Dark):**
- Workers return exhausted
- Families prepare meager meals
- Secret gatherings (risky but happen)
- Some pray, some plot

**Night (After Dark):**
- Curfew enforced strictly
- Patrols with lanterns
- Violators arrested or beaten
- Underground resistance meets in shadows

## **Factions Within Village**

### **Order Loyalists (~30%):**
**Who:** Wealthy landowners, devout families, practical survivors
**View:** "Better Order than chaos. At least we're safe from Maelstrom."
**Actions:** Report suspicious activity, attend services eagerly, kiss up to templars
**Benefits:** Extra rations, better treatment, protection from harassment

**Key Loyalists:**
- **Elder Garen Whitefield:** Village spokesman, collaborator
- **Priestess Marta:** Enthusiastic Order convert
- **Merchant Donal:** Profits from Order supply contracts

### **Silent Resenters (~50%):**
**Who:** Most farmers, craftspeople, older residents
**View:** "We hate this but what can we do? Survive and endure."
**Actions:** Comply outwardly, resist passively, hide what they can
**Survival:** Keep heads down, hide extra food, protect families

**Typical Villagers:**
- **Farmer Elsa:** Lost husband to conscription, barely feeding kids
- **Blacksmith Tormund:** Forced to make Order weapons, hates it
- **Baker Lila:** Waters down bread to stretch flour rations

### **Active Resisters (~15%):**
**Who:** Young adults, those who lost everything, hot-heads
**View:** "We won't be slaves. We'll fight even if we die trying."
**Actions:** Sabotage, theft, smuggling, contacts with outside factions
**Risks:** Execution if caught, reprisals against village

**Resistance Cell (Secret):**
- **"Reaper's Hand":** 8-12 members
- Led by **Jorin the Miller's Son**
- Hide food from requisitions
- Help refugees escape to [[üõ°Ô∏è Pragmatic Coalition]]
- Sabotage Order equipment subtly
- Pass intelligence to anti-Order factions

### **Refugees (~5% but visible):**
**Who:** Maelstrom survivors, displaced families, desperate wanderers
**View:** "We just want to live. We'll do anything for food."
**Situation:** Given worst tasks, minimal rations, treated as burden
**Tensions:** Villagers resent them taking scarce food
**Vulnerability:** Prime targets for [[üåÄ Ascendant Cult]] recruitment

## **DM Notes**

### **Running Goldmeadow:**
- Show the human cost of occupation
- Make villagers sympathetic but complicated
- Order templars aren't all evil‚Äîsome troubled by duty
- Starvation is real‚Äîdescribe emaciated children
- Create moral dilemmas with no easy answers

### **Faction Balance:**
- Don't make Order cartoon villains
- Show why some villagers support them (fear, belief, pragmatism)
- Resistance isn't purely good‚Äîsabotage hurts village too
- Cult offers tempting alternative to suffering

### **Consequences:**
- Player choices should matter significantly
- Help resistance = Order crackdown
- Help Order = villagers suffer more
- Ignore situation = things get worse
- No perfect solution exists

### **Atmosphere:**
- Tension everywhere‚Äîfear, hunger, resentment
- Beautiful fields hiding ugly reality
- Children's laughter rare and precious
- Constant presence of armed templars
- Whispered conversations, furtive glances
