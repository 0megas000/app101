**Type:** Major River System | **Length:** ~180 miles from source to delta **Width:** 200-400 feet (varies by section) | **Depth:** 15-30 feet (navigable) **Strategic Value:** Critical - Trade, Water Supply, Transportation **Corruption Status:** Partially Affected **Primary Settlements:** [[‚öì Haven's Rest]], multiple smaller towns

## **Overview**

Once the lifeblood of three-kingdom trade, the Silverflow River earned its name from the distinctive shimmer of its waters‚Äîa silvery gleam caused by mineral deposits from mountain springs mixing with pure glacial melt. For centuries, merchant barges laden with goods floated down from the [[‚õ∞Ô∏è Ironspine Mountains]] to the great port of [[‚öì Haven's Rest]], then out to the sea.

The Maelstrom changed everything. The river now flows through corrupted territory, its upper reaches still pristine while its middle section passes dangerously close to reality-warping chaos. Trade has collapsed to a fraction of pre-crisis levels, but the river remains vital‚Äîas transportation route, water source, natural barrier, and contested strategic asset.

## **Geography & Course**

### **Source - The Crystal Springs** _(Ironspine Mountains)_

**Elevation:** 8,000 feet **Character:** Dozens of mountain springs merge into fast-flowing streams **Water Quality:** Pure, cold, crystal-clear **Hazards:** Rapids, waterfalls, narrow channels **Navigation:** Impossible for boats, dangerous even for rafts

### **Upper River - The Mountain Run** _(Miles 0-40)_

**Flow:** Fast, turbulent, rocky **Width:** 50-100 feet **Water Quality:** Clean, drinkable, cold **Settlements:** Small mountain villages, mining outposts **Navigation:** Small boats only, skilled pilots required **Control:** [[‚õ∞Ô∏è Ironhold]] patrols sporadically

- **Narrow Gates:** Canyon section where river squeezes through rock
- **Mining Runoff:** Some tributaries carry mineral sediment
- **Logging Camps:** Timber floated downstream from here

### **Middle River - The Trade Reach** _(Miles 40-120)_

**Flow:** Moderate, wide, deep **Width:** 200-300 feet **Water Quality:** Clean but approaching danger zone **Settlements:** Several river towns (mostly evacuated or struggling) **Navigation:** Cargo vessels, passenger boats, military craft **Control:** Contested by multiple factions

- **Corrupted Debris:** Wreckage phasing in and out of existence

## **The Silverflow Shimmer** _(Why It's Silver)_

The distinctive silvery appearance comes from:

- **Mountain Minerals:** Mica and quartz particles suspended in water
- **Glacial Melt:** Carries fine rock powder reflecting light
- **Ancient Magic:** Pre-kingdom enchantments for safe passage (mostly faded)
- **Natural Phenomenon:** Specific angle of sunlight hits mineral content

**In corrupted zones:** The shimmer turns purple-silver, sometimes shows images of things that aren't there, or reflects places from other dimensions.

## **River Traffic & Trade**

### **Pre-Crisis** _(Historical)_

- 200+ merchant vessels operating daily
- Constant flow of timber, ore, grain, manufactured goods
- Passenger ferries connecting all settlements
- Fishing fleet of 500+ boats
- River was safer and faster than roads

### **Current State** _(Crisis Era)_

- 20-30 vessels brave the full route per week
- Most traffic limited to safe sections
- Desperate merchants pay premium prices
- Military vessels patrol but can't secure everything
- Smugglers thrive in chaos

### **Vessel Types:**

**Cargo Barges:** Flat-bottomed, slow, carry heavy loads **Swift Traders:** Smaller, faster, risky but profitable **Refugee Boats:** Overcrowded, barely seaworthy, heartbreaking **Military Patrol Craft:** Armed, armored, escort duty **Fishing Vessels:** Local craft, stay in safe zones **Smuggler Skiffs:** Fast, hidden compartments, neutral flags

## **The Sirens of Silverflow**

### **Origin:**

Nobody knows if sirens always lived in the river or if they're drawn by the Maelstrom's corruption. They appear exclusively in the Crisis Zone, attracted to chaos, fear, and death.

### **Appearance:**

- **Humanoid Upper Body:** Beautiful but wrong‚Äîskin too pale, eyes too large
- **Fish-Like Lower Body:** Scaled tail, can be iridescent or dark
- **Unnatural Movement:** Swim against currents, hover above water
- **Voice:** Hauntingly beautiful, carries for miles
- **Reality Distortion:** Shimmer around them like heat waves

### **Behavior:**

- **Ambush Predators:** Hide in reality eddies or among wreckage
- **Luring Song:** Enchanting melody draws victims into water
- **Pack Hunters:** Usually 3-6 working together
- **Intelligent:** Can assess threats, retreat when outmatched
- **Corruption-Touched:** Show signs of transformation, more aggressive than folklore suggests

### **The Siren Song:**

**Effect:** Those who hear must resist being drawn toward the water **Range:** Up to 500 feet **Content:** Promises of safety, rest, answers, loved ones **Victims:** Dive into water, swim toward sirens willingly **Result:** Drowning, devoured, or dragged to unknown fate

**Protection:**

- Wax in ears (traditional but reduces awareness)
- Magical silence or protection from charm
- Strong will and companions to restrain
- Loud noise to drown out singing
- [[üö∂ The Pilgrims]] claim immunity (corruption adaptation)

### **Encounters:**

Sirens attack primarily at:

- **Dawn/Dusk:** Low light, hard to see them
- **Foggy Conditions:** Hide in mist over water
- **Narrow Channels:** Vessels can't maneuver to escape
- **Wrecked Ships:** Prey on survivors

**Tactics:**

1. Song draws victims to water's edge or overboard
2. Pull victims under while enchanted
3. If resisted, use claws and magic (water control)
4. Retreat if facing organized defense
5. Sometimes work with [[üåÄ Ascendant Cult]] (unclear if alliance or coincidence)

## **Notable Locations Along River**

### **Ironbridge** _(Upper River)_

Small town at river's first navigable point. Active but struggling. Acts as transfer point where mountain goods meet river boats.

### **The Broken Bridge** _(Crisis Zone)_

Massive stone bridge once connected main trade roads. Now partially phased into another dimension‚Äîeastern half exists, western half flickers in and out. Treacherous crossing point.

### **Wreck Reef** _(Crisis Zone)_

Graveyard of abandoned vessels. Some from pre-crisis, some recent failures. Sirens nest here. Valuable salvage if you're brave/foolish.

### **Pilgrim's Ford** _(Crisis Zone)_

Shallow section where [[üö∂ The Pilgrims]] can wade across safely. They guide others for a price. One of few reliable crossing points.

### **Haven's Rest** _(Lower River)_

Major port city, Coalition headquarters, refugee center, and commercial hub. The river's reason for existing as trade route.

## **River Hazards**

### **Natural:**

- **Rapids:** Upper river especially dangerous
- **Sandbars:** Shift position, ground vessels
- **Weather:** Storms cause flooding and dangerous currents
- **Ice:** Winter freezes upper sections
- **Fog:** Common in delta, reduces visibility to nothing

### **Corruption-Related:**

- **Reality Eddies:** Whirlpools leading elsewhere
- **Dimensional Drift:** Vessel phases partially out of reality
- **Time Distortion:** Journey takes wrong amount of time
- **Phantom Ships:** Encounter vessels that shouldn't exist
- **Water Transformation:** Drinking causes mutations

### **Hostile:**

- **Sirens:** Crisis Zone ambush predators
- **Pirates:** Opportunistic raiders
- **Faction Conflict:** Wrong flag = attacked
- **Cultists:** [[üåÄ Ascendant Cult]] river camps
- **Mutated Wildlife:** Aggressive corrupted fish, river serpents

## **Travel Times & Routes**

### **Full River Journey:**

**Downstream (with current):** 7-10 days from mountains to sea **Upstream (against current):** 14-20 days, exhausting **Crisis Zone Passage:** 2 days downstream, 4 days upstream (if you survive)

### **Common Routes:**

**Ironhold to Haven's Rest:** Full journey, rare but possible **Haven's Rest to Safe Zone Edge:** Regular trade route **Short Hauls:** Between nearby settlements in safe zones **Smuggler Runs:** Crisis Zone shortcuts (deadly but profitable)

### **Escort Services:**

**Coalition Convoys:** Weekly scheduled, heavily armed, 50gp per vessel **Pilgrim Guides:** Know safe paths through Crisis Zone, 100gp per crossing **Mercenary Guards:** Variable quality, 20-200gp depending on threat

## **DM Notes**

### **Running River Adventures:**

- Use weather to create tension (fog, storms)
- Siren encounters should be atmospheric before combat
- Emphasize feeling of vulnerability on water
- Show economic impact‚Äîtrade goods, refugee desperation
- Make Crisis Zone genuinely dangerous to cross

### **Sirens:**

- Roleplaying opportunity‚Äîwhat do they sing about?
- Make song specific to character fears/desires
- Don't overuse‚Äîkeep them scary
- Consider what happens to survivors they capture

### **Strategic Layer:**

- River control affects faction power
- Players' escort choices have consequences
- Reputation matters‚Äîhelp Coalition, anger Order, etc.
- Economic impact of protecting/disrupting trade

### **Corruption Effects:**

- Gradual increase as approach Crisis Zone
- Let players notice changes in water, wildlife, light
- Use random encounter tables for variety
- Show corruption spreading slowly downstream
