# The Golden Citadel

**Type:** Fortress-Cathedral | **Location:** Highest Hill, Goldspire  
**Faction:** Vigilant Order (Headquarters) | **Population:** 3,500 (1,200 templars, 2,300 support)  
**Defenses:** Fortified (DC 20 to breach) | **Threat Level:** Extreme (hostile to heretics)

## Overview

Massive cathedral-fortress of white marble with golden spires and steel reinforcements. Once Goldspire's grandest bank, Aurelius Goldmantle transformed it into the Vigilant Order's militarized seat of power. Banking vaults now store weapons and divine artifacts, prayer halls function as war rooms, and every golden altar doubles as a symbol of divine authority.

## Key Features

**Physical Appearance:**

- White marble walls reinforced with blessed steel
- Seven golden spires visible for miles (tallest: 200 feet)
- Crystal windows depicting Thyros the Ender's glory
- Fortified gates (AC 19, 500 HP, immune to fire/radiant)
- Courtyard fitting 1,000 soldiers

**Interior:**

- Former bank vaults converted to armories (DC 25 to breach)
- Grand prayer hall seats 2,000 (now war planning center)
- Aurelius Goldmantle's throne room (top spire)
- Templar barracks (organized by rank)
- Underground prison cells (60 capacity)

## D&D 5e Mechanics

**Entering the Citadel:**

- **Invited Guests:** Escorted at all times, DC 15 Insight to notice surveillance
- **Sneaking In:** DC 22 Stealth (Advantage if disguised as templar)
- **Forced Entry:** Requires siege equipment or 9th-level magic
- **Social:** DC 18 Persuasion or proof of faith to gain audience

**Combat Encounters:**

- **Templars (Guards):** CR 3 (6-12 per patrol)
- **High Inquisitors:** CR 8 (3-5 available)
- **Aurelius Goldmantle:** CR 15 (if provoked)
- **Divine Wards:** Advantage on saves vs. undead/fiends within citadel

**Notable Defenses:**

- Templars patrol every 10 minutes (2d4 per patrol)
- Divine sensors detect evil (DC 20 Deception to mask)
- Alarm spells on all entry points
- Scrying focus in throne room

**Loot & Resources:**

- Armory: 200 longswords, 150 shields, 50 crossbows (all blessed)
- Treasury: 50,000 GP in offerings (heavily guarded)
- Library: Religious texts, heresy records, corruption research
- Holy Relics: 2d4 uncommon divine items, 1 rare relic

## Key Locations

**First Floor - Public Access:**

- Grand Hall of Judgment (trials, pronouncements)
- Pilgrims' Chapel (commoners pray here)
- Offering Chambers (donations accepted)
- Guard Posts (24/7 vigilance)

**Second Floor - Administrative:**

- War Planning Chamber (faction strategy)
- Records Archive (heresy documentation)
- Council Chamber (High Inquisitors meet)
- Messenger Station (ravens, sending stones)

**Third Floor - Restricted:**

- High Inquisitor Quarters (15 private rooms)
- Divine Artifact Vault (DC 25 to open)
- Interrogation Chambers (dark rituals)
- Secret Passages (DC 20 Investigation to find)

**Top Spire - Aurelius's Domain:**

- Throne Room (private audiences)
- Personal Library (ancient texts)
- Meditation Chamber (scrying focus)
- Escape Route (teleportation circle)

**Underground - The Depths:**

- Armory Vaults (blessed weapons cache)
- Prison Complex (60 cells, DC 20 to escape)
- Execution Chamber (grim duty)
- Old Bank Vault (divine relics, 100,000 GP reserve)

## NPCs Present

**Aurelius Goldmantle** (High Inquisitor, CR 15)

- AC 20 (plate + shield), 180 HP, Paladin 15
- Divine Smite, Aura of Protection, Compelled Duel
- Personality: Zealous, charismatic, ruthless against heresy
- Goal: Eliminate corruption at any cost

**Cardinal-Treasurer Matthias Vaultkeeper** (Financial Leader, CR 8)

- AC 15 (robes of protection), 90 HP, Cleric 9
- Controls vast wealth, funds operations
- Personality: Calculating, pragmatic, values order
- Goal: Fund crusade against Ascendant Cult

**Sister-Marshall Vex Ironhold** (Military Commander, CR 10)

- AC 18 (half-plate), 130 HP, Fighter 11
- Commands 1,200 templars
- Personality: Disciplined, tactical, loyal
- Goal: Fortify borders, prepare for holy war

## Daily Schedule

**Dawn:** Mass prayer (all templars), divine blessings cast  
**Morning:** Patrol rotations, training drills (courtyard)  
**Midday:** War council, strategic planning  
**Afternoon:** Trials, interrogations, punishments  
**Evening:** Officer meetings, intelligence reports  
**Night:** Reduced guard (DC 18 Stealth to infiltrate)

## Faction Relations

**Allied:** None (trust only themselves)  
**Hostile:** Ascendant Cult, Empirical Academy (heretics)  
**Tense:** Pragmatic Coalition (question their methods)  
**Suspicious:** Divine Awakeners (unknown agenda), Pilgrims (too neutral)

## Plot Hooks

1. **Infiltration Mission:** Steal heresy records before trial
2. **Prison Break:** Extract wrongly accused prisoner
3. **Assassinate Aurelius:** Rival faction's desperate plan
4. **Retrieve Relic:** Divine artifact locked in vault
5. **False Accusation:** Party branded heretics, must clear names
6. **Inside Information:** Recruit sympathetic templar as spy
7. **Siege Defense:** Help or hinder Order during cult attack
8. **Divine Artifact Hunt:** Both sides seek same relic in vault

## DM Notes

**Running the Citadel:**

- Emphasize oppressive authority and religious fervor
- Templars are disciplined, not stupid - they coordinate
- Aurelius is dangerous but not insane - he believes he's righteous
- Gold and marble contrast with brutal interrogations

**Skill Challenges:**

- Stealth (infiltration), Persuasion (talk way in), Deception (disguise)
- Religion (pass as faithful), Intimidation (command templars)
- Investigation (find secrets), Athletics (scale walls)

**Consequences:**

- Caught: Interrogation, trial, execution or imprisonment
- Success: Vigilant Order becomes enemy or suspicious
- Escape: Templars hunt party across region
