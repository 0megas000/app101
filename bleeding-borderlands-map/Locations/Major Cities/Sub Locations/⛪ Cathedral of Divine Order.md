# Cathedral of Divine Order

**Type:** Cathedral (Vigilant Order) | **Location:** Central Goldspire  
**Size:** Massive (350 ft tall, holds 3,000) | **Defenses:** Fortified, blessed wards  
**Faction:** Vigilant Order | **Threat Level:** High (hostile to heretics)

## Overview

The spiritual heart of the Vigilant Order, this imposing cathedral dominates Goldspire's skyline with its seven golden spires. Built centuries ago as a temple to Thyros the Ender, it now serves as the Order's primary place of worship, indoctrination, and public displays of power.

## Key Features

**Architecture:**

- White marble walls with golden trim
- Seven spires representing divine perfection
- Crystal windows depicting Thyros's final judgment
- Bronze doors (AC 19, 300 HP, blessed)
- Capacity: 3,000 worshippers

**Main Chamber:**

- Altar of Thyros (blessed, radiates divine energy)
- Elevated pulpit for High Inquisitor sermons
- Pews for congregation (strict hierarchy by rank)
- Confession booths (12 total, some monitored)
- Acoustic design amplifies speaker's voice

**Underground:**

- Crypts of Past Inquisitors (10 interred, guarded)
- Relic Vault (DC 25 to breach, contains holy artifacts)
- Interrogation Chambers (secret, soundproofed)
- Tunnel to Golden Citadel (emergency escape)

## D&D 5e Mechanics

**Divine Wards:**

- Advantage on saves vs. undead/fiends within cathedral
- Hallow spell (permanent) in main chamber
- Detect Evil and Good always active (DC 20 Deception to hide)
- Alarm spells on all entry points

**Entry & Access:**

- **Public Access:** Main chamber during services (dawn/midday/dusk)
- **Restricted:** Side chapels require templar escort
- **Forbidden:** Underground areas (DC 22 Stealth to infiltrate)
- **Sneaking In:** DC 20 Stealth during services (distracted guards)

**Services & Schedule:**

- **Dawn Prayer:** 500-800 templars + 200-400 citizens
- **Midday Sermon:** Smaller gathering, announcements
- **Dusk Vigil:** Largest service, 1,500-2,000 attendees
- **Special Events:** Trials, inquisitions, executions

**NPCs Present:**

- High Priest Valorian (CR 8, Cleric 10)
- 6 Assistant Priests (CR 4, Cleric 5)
- 12 Templar Guards on rotation (CR 3, Paladin 5)
- 4 Altar Attendants (CR 1/2, commoners)

## Key Locations

**Ground Floor:**

- Main worship chamber (3,000 capacity)
- Confession booths (privacy, some compromised)
- Side chapels (small prayer rooms, 12 total)
- Donation hall (where citizens give offerings)

**Upper Galleries:**

- Choir loft (overlooks main chamber)
- Bell tower (7 bells, signal alarm)
- High Priest's office (administrative)
- Private prayer chambers (High Inquisitors only)

**Crypts Below:**

- Tombs of past leadership (10 crypts, guarded by wights)
- Relic Vault (3 rare artifacts, 8 uncommon)
- Secret interrogation rooms (soundproofed)
- Escape tunnel to Golden Citadel (1 mile, trapped)

## Plot Hooks

1. **Steal Relic:** Artifact needed from vault
2. **Expose Secrets:** Find evidence of corruption in leadership
3. **Interrupt Trial:** Save innocent from public execution
4. **Confession Espionage:** Overhear crucial information
5. **Underground Escape:** Rescue prisoner from crypts
6. **Disrupt Service:** Create distraction during packed ceremony
7. **Holy Artifact:** Commission clerics to bless item
8. **Sanctuary:** Claim right of sanctuary from enemies

## DM Notes

**Atmosphere:**

- Oppressive grandeur, designed to inspire awe/fear
- Echo of footsteps in vast space
- Incense smoke, chanting, golden light through crystal
- Sense of being watched (it's true - spies everywhere)

**Combat Encounters:**

- Templars don't fight alone - they sound alarm
- 2d6 templars arrive in 1d4 rounds after alarm
- High Priest casts buff spells before engaging
- Undead guardians rise in crypts if disturbed
