## **Overview**
A towering colossus of stone and ice that dominates the horizon for a hundred miles in every direction. Mount Thyros is the highest peak in the known world, its summit perpetually shrouded in clouds that locals call "the Veil of Finality." The mountain is beautiful in its severity‚Äîsharp, unforgiving, absolute. No birds circle its peaks. No plants cling to its slopes above the tree line. Only snow, stone, and silence.

Deep within its frozen heart lies the tomb of [[‚ö∞Ô∏è Thyros the Ender]], god of death and finality, sealed away in an ancient chamber of sacrifice and eternal rest.

## **Physical Appearance**

### **The Mountain:**
- **Massive Scale:** 18,000+ feet of sheer stone and ice
- **Sharp Profile:** Angular, intimidating silhouette against the sky
- **Eternal Snow:** Peaks white year-round, glaciers on every face
- **Black Stone:** Dark granite visible through snow, almost obsidian
- **Vertical Faces:** Sheer cliffs make climbing treacherous
- **Avalanche Prone:** Constant danger from shifting snow
- **The Summit:** Impossible to see through perpetual cloud cover

### **The Approach:**
- **Lower Slopes (0-8,000 ft):** Rocky foothills with sparse pine forests
- **Middle Mountain (8,000-14,000 ft):** Open snowfields and ice formations
- **Upper Reaches (14,000-18,000 ft):** Deadly conditions, thin air, brutal winds
- **The Tomb Entrance:** Hidden at ~12,000 feet on the northwestern face

### **Atmosphere:**
- **Oppressive Cold:** Not just temperature‚Äîcold that seeps into your soul
- **Rigid Silence:** Sound feels wrong, muffled, final
- **Sense of Doom:** Not terror, but acceptance‚Äî"this is where things end"
- **Timelessness:** Like the mountain has always been and always will be
- **Watchfulness:** Feeling of being judged for worthiness

## **The Climb**

### **Lower Slopes (Days 1-2):**
**Difficulty:** Moderate
**Hazards:**
- Loose scree and unstable rock
- Hidden crevasses under thin snow
- Sudden weather changes
- Mountain predators (bears, wolves)

**Encounters:**
- Normal wildlife avoiding the mountain
- Other expedition camps
- Warning markers from previous climbers
- Abandoned gear from failed attempts

### **Middle Mountain (Days 3-4):**
**Difficulty:** Hard
**Hazards:**
- Severe altitude effects (exhaustion, disorientation)
- Blizzard conditions with zero visibility
- Avalanche danger zones
- Crevasses hundreds of feet deep

**Encounters:**
- **Snow Giants:** 12-foot tall humanoids made of animated ice and stone
- **Ice Golems:** Smaller constructs patrolling specific areas
- Frozen corpses of previous climbers (intact, eyes open)
- Strange ice formations that seem deliberately placed

**Snow Giants & Golems:**
- Not intelligent, but purposeful
- Guard approaches to tomb entrance
- Attack anything that comes too close
- Immune to cold, resistant to physical damage
- Can summon blizzards and avalanches
- 3-5 giants patrol set routes
- 8-12 golems serve as sentries

### **Upper Reaches (Day 5+):**
**Difficulty:** Nearly Impossible
**Hazards:**
- Oxygen deprivation
- Temperatures of -40¬∞F or lower
- Winds that can knock people off cliffs
- Flash-freezing fog that causes frostbite in seconds
- Hallucinations from altitude

**Encounters:**
- **Greater Snow Giants:** 20-foot tall patrol leaders
- Reality feels thin‚Äîdeath seems very close
- Visions of your own death (prophetic warnings)
- The pull to just "rest" and never wake up

## **The Tomb Entrance**

**Location:** Northwestern face, ~12,000 feet elevation
**Appearance:** Natural cave mouth, 15 feet tall, framed by icicles like teeth
**Discovery:** Easy to miss‚Äîblends with surrounding rock formations
**Markers:** Ancient runes carved into stone, faded and worn

### **The Cave System:**
**Entry Passage:** 200 feet of twisting natural tunnel, narrow and claustrophobic
**The Descent:** Spiraling pathway leads down 300 feet into the mountain's heart
**Temperature:** Somehow colder inside than outside
**Light:** None‚Äîtotal darkness except what you bring
**Sound:** Echoes strangely, as if space is larger than it seems

### **Guardian Undead:**
Stationed throughout the cave system, these are not mindless zombies but eternal sentinels.

**Types:**
- **Tomb Wardens (6):** Armored skeletons with ancient weapons, skilled combatants
- **Bone Knights (3):** Elite warriors in rusted full plate, devastating fighters  
- **Death Priests (2):** Robed figures channeling necrotic energy, spellcasters
- **The Keeper:** Single powerful undead guardian at tomb entrance (see below)

**Behavior:**
- Patient‚Äîwill wait centuries between encounters
- Intelligent‚Äîcan assess threats and tactics
- Unyielding‚Äîfight to complete destruction
- Silent‚Äîcommunicate only through action
- Purpose-driven‚Äîexist solely to protect tomb

**Combat Notes:**
- Coordinate attacks intelligently
- Use terrain advantage (narrow passages, ambush points)
- Necrotic damage and life drain abilities
- Resistant to non-magical weapons
- Can sense living beings entering the cave
- Do not pursue beyond cave entrance

## **The Tomb Chamber** *(Deep Inside)*

### **The Approach:**
Final passage opens into massive natural cavern, at least 200 feet across and 100 feet high. The temperature drops even further. Your breath freezes instantly. The air feels heavy with finality.

### **The Keeper:**
Before entering the main chamber, adventurers face **The Keeper**‚Äîa single undead guardian of immense power.

**Appearance:** 8-foot tall armored figure, ancient plate blackened and covered in frost. Empty eye sockets burn with pale blue fire. Carries a massive two-handed sword etched with runes of ending.

**Power Level:** Deadly encounter for even high-level parties
**Abilities:** 
- Necrotic aura that drains life
- Can summon shadows and specters
- Immune to cold, resistant to most damage
- Regenerates unless completely destroyed
- Speaks (first time any undead has): "Turn back. This is not your ending."

**The Test:** The Keeper evaluates worthiness. Those with pure intent to understand or protect (not exploit) may be allowed to pass. Those seeking power or disturbance must fight.

### **The Chamber Layout:**

**The Floor:** Perfectly circular, 150 feet diameter, carved from single piece of black stone. Surface polished mirror-smooth despite millennia.

**The Empty Tombs (24 total):**
Arranged in perfect circle around the chamber's perimeter. Each is human-sized, carved from white marble, lids open and empty‚Äîwaiting for occupants who never came.

**Purpose:** Meant for ritual sacrifices to seal Thyros. The sealing was either completed differently or never completed. Evidence suggests last-minute change of plans.

**Details:**
- Names carved on each tomb (languages lost to time)
- Burial goods still present (ancient coins, weapons, jewelry)
- No bodies‚Äînever were any, or bodies removed?
- Faint magical residue suggests powerful binding ritual

**The Central Casket:**
**Size:** 30 feet long, 15 feet wide, 10 feet tall‚Äîas large as a small house
**Material:** Single piece of black stone, same as floor
**Appearance:** Perfectly rectangular, no ornamentation except single symbol on top‚Äîa circle bisected by a line (beginning and ending)
**Condition:** Sealed with what appears to be divine magic, completely intact

**Approaching the Casket:**
- Overwhelming sense of finality and peace
- All sound becomes muted to near-silence
- Time feels like it stops
- Breathing becomes difficult (not physically‚Äîexistentially)
- Compulsion to simply lie down and rest forever

**Inside (If Opened):**
[[‚ö∞Ô∏è Thyros the Ender]] lies in state, appearing as a tall humanoid figure in simple robes, hands folded, completely still. Neither alive nor dead‚Äîsuspended in the moment between. Ancient texts say waking Thyros will bring "the end of endings"‚Äîbut what that means, no one knows.

## **Treasures & Artifacts**

### **In the Empty Tombs:**
- Ancient weapons (masterwork quality, magical)
- Burial jewelry (valuable, some with minor enchantments)
- Coins from pre-kingdom era (historically priceless)
- Personal effects (journals in dead languages, portraits)
- Ritual components (rare materials for powerful magic)

### **In the Chamber:**
- Death-aspected magic items (necrotic focus, life-draining weapons)
- Fragments of binding rituals (knowledge of divine containment)
- The Keeper's sword (legendary weapon if earned through honor)
- Runes of Finality (can end magical effects permanently)

### **Thyros's Tomb (if opened):**
- **The Shroud of Ending:** Cloth covering god, grants immunity to death magic
- **The Final Word:** Artifact that can permanently kill even gods
- **Crown of Silence:** Grants power over undead and death itself
- **Thyros's Blessing:** Touch from a death god‚Äîcomplicated consequences

**Warning:** Taking artifacts from tomb may break seal, wake Thyros, or attract divine attention from other powers.

## **Environmental Challenges**

### **Cold:**
- Mundane cold resistance not enough above 10,000 feet
- Magical protection required for tomb interior
- Frostbite damage accumulates over time
- Equipment freezes and becomes brittle

### **Altitude:**
- Exhaustion from thin air
- Constitution saves to avoid altitude sickness
- Disadvantage on physical checks above 14,000 feet
- Healing less effective in low oxygen

### **Weather:**
- Blizzards reduce visibility to zero
- Avalanches triggered by loud noises or tremors
- Wind strong enough to knock people prone or off cliffs
- Sudden storms trap expeditions for days

### **Navigation:**
- Easy to get lost in whiteout conditions
- Landmarks buried or changed by snowfall
- Compass interference near tomb (divine energy)
- Time distortion‚Äîdays feel like hours or vice versa
