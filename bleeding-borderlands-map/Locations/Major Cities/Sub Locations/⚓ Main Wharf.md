# Main Wharf

**Type:** Port Facility | **Location:** Haven's Rest (Riverfront)  
**Size:** Massive (handles 12 large barges) | **Activity:** Constant  
**Faction:** Pragmatic Coalition (controlled) | **Workers:** 500+

## Overview

Bustling river port handling the logistical backbone of refugee relief. Barges arrive hourly loaded with supplies, refugees, and trade goods. Cranes swing, dockhands shout, and the organized chaos never stops. This is where survival operations physically happen.

## Key Features

**Docking Facilities:**

- 12 barge berths (2d6 occupied at any time)
- Loading cranes (mechanical, operated by teams)
- Warehouse row (8 buildings, storage capacity 50,000 cubic ft each)
- Customs house (inspections, paperwork)

**Support Infrastructure:**

- Harbormaster's office (schedules, fees, regulations)
- Dockworker guild hall (labor coordination)
- Repair dry-dock (vessel maintenance)
- Cargo scales (weighted inspection)

**Security:**

- 20 dock guards (CR 1, patrol constantly)
- Harbor patrol boats (2, crewed by 4 each)
- Customs inspectors (detect smuggling DC 18)

## D&D 5e Mechanics

**Smuggling:** DC 18 Sleight of Hand to hide item, DC 18 Deception to pass inspection  
**Bribes:** 50-200 GP depending on cargo  
**Stowaway:** DC 15 Stealth to board, DC 18 to remain hidden during voyage  
**Cargo Theft:** DC 20 Stealth (heavily patrolled)

**River Travel:**

- Passenger fare: 5 SP per 10 miles
- Private charter: 10 GP per day
- Cargo shipping: 1 GP per 100 lbs per 50 miles

## NPCs

**Harbormaster Torvin** (CR 3, gruff but fair)  
**Guild Boss Marta** (CR 4, controls dockworker labor)  
**20 Dock Guards** (CR 1 each)  
**200+ Dockworkers** (CR 0, commoners)

## Plot Hooks

- Smuggle contraband past customs
- Intercept enemy spy arriving by barge
- Investigate cargo theft ring
- Protect important shipment
- Chase enemy escaping by boat
